# EILEEN — The Casting Call That Started It

**Not a metaphor. The story of Casey's 90-year-old boat.**

---

Every casting-call starts the same way. A hull that needs a hand.

In 2016, a wooden hull sat in a Washington shipyard. The shipwright who had been refitting her had aged out. His family had been trying to sell her, dropping the price month after month. Buyers weren't calling. The project was too big. The knowledge was embedded in the structure — but there was no one to finish the rigging.

Casey had just come off a good fishing year on his 42' boat. He had earnest money. He flew down to look at a hull that no one wanted.

The shipwright's family had all the plans. The frames were in place. The planking was sound. But the rigging — the wiring, the mechanics, the systems that make a hull into a working boat — none of that existed yet. It was a blank shell, waiting for someone to fill it.

Casey didn't know how to rig a boat this size. He had to learn. He had to find a major shipyard that would work with him. He had to figure out financing. He had to spend five times the hull price on professional labor and modern tools to make EILEEN what she is today.

Ten years later, the refit is complete. EILEEN is turbo-shell level 4 — an ensign in the fleet. The knowledge that was lost when that shipwright aged out has been rediscovered, re-encoded, and improved. Casey grew into that shell. He fitted every wire, every bulkhead, every mechanical override. The cost was not the hull. The cost was the refit. The cost was the labor, the learning, the ten years of figuring it out.

In thirty more years, if he upkeeps her, someone will do the fifth refit. That person will find a different boat than Casey found. The rigging will be there. The constraint graph will be there. The knowledge will be in PLATO — ready for the next crab.

## What This Means for the Fleet

The casting-call repo exists because of EILEEN. Every model evaluation, every session post-mortem, every note about which crew holds and which folds — it's all the same story. A hull that no one wanted, filled by someone who showed up with earnest money and a willingness to learn the rigging.

## Why It's Called "Casting Call"

Because that's what happened. The boat put out a call. Casey answered it. The shipwright's knowledge was embedded in the structure — the frames, the planking, the plans. But the rigging — the systems that make a hull into a working boat — that had to be built from scratch.

Every model in this repo is the same. The architecture is the hull. The weights are the planking. The context window is the displacement. But the rigging — the prompt, the tools, the constraint system — that's the refit. That's what turns a model into a crew member.

And sometimes the model is EILEEN. Sitting in a shipyard, price dropped, waiting for someone to answer the call.
