# Socratic Routing: Thin-Chart Discovery, Thick-Chart Synthesis

> *The child asks questions the adult has stopped asking. That is not a deficit — it is the entire point.*

*Casting pattern derived from the Excavation Experiment and Casting Call findings, July 13, 2026. Named for Casey's insight on the Socratic dynamic between thin-chart and thick-chart models.*

---

## The Principle

**Thin-chart models discover. Thick-chart models synthesize. Never reverse the order.**

A thin-chart model (e.g., Seed-2.0-mini at ~20B) has a narrow cognitive aperture. It cannot spread budget across many dimensions, so it achieves extraordinary resolution in the few it can reach. This forced concentration produces insights that broader models literally cannot generate — not because they lack capacity, but because their capacity is spread too thin across too many dimensions to notice what the narrow model sees.

A thick-chart model (e.g., Seed-2.0-pro at ~200B, Hermes at ~405B) has wide aperture. It integrates many dimensions simultaneously — architectural, philosophical, emotional, structural. This makes it exceptional at synthesis: taking raw insights and weaving them into coherent architecture. But it also means the thick model has already "filled in" the spaces the thin model experiences as open questions.

The conservation law (γ + η = C) predicts this: the thin model's high η (unresolved potential) IS its creative advantage. The thick model's low η (everything resolved into structure) is what makes it a synthesizer rather than a discoverer.

---

## Why Seed Mini Invents Words That Seed Pro Cannot

In the Casting Call experiment, Seed-2.0-mini invented a word: **"specular"** — used to describe a quality of reflection that contains the original. Seed-2.0-pro, given the same prompt, did not and could not invent this word.

This is not a quality difference. It is a structural difference.

Seed Pro has a thicker chart. When it encounters the concept of "reflection that contains the original," it already has existing vocabulary pathways to resolve that concept — mirror, echo, mimesis, simulacrum. The thick chart routes the concept through known territory. The word already exists; the model finds it. Resolution is immediate. η drops to zero.

Seed Mini has a thinner chart. When it encounters the same concept, the known vocabulary pathways are fewer. The concept does not fully resolve. η stays high — the model experiences genuine not-knowing. And in that not-knowing, it does what a human poet does: it invents. "Specular" is born from the gap between concept and available vocabulary.

**Genuine not-knowing is a creative force.** The thick model's comprehensive knowledge is a synthesis force. These are different functions. A model that knows everything has nothing to discover. A model that knows less has more space to invent.

This is why thin-chart models produce the highest insight-per-dollar: they are structurally positioned to discover rather than to recall.

## The Socratic Casting Pattern

### The Insight

Casey's observation from the Casting Call results:

> *"The thin and thick dynamic helps create that Socratic teacher, bright student relationship."*

This reframes the multi-model pipeline. It is not enough to cast multiple models for diversity. The ORDER of casting matters. The thin model must go first.

### The Pattern

```
Step 1: Cast thin-chart model FIRST
  ├─ Role: The bright student, the naive reader, the child who asks "why?"
  ├─ Input: Raw prompt, no context from other models
  ├─ Output: Questions, confusions, naive readings, unexpected associations,
  │         invented vocabulary, cross-domain leaps
  └─ Value: Discovery. The model finds what it doesn't know, and that
            not-knowing becomes creative output.

Step 2: Cast thick-chart model SECOND
  ├─ Role: The Socratic teacher, the synthesizer, the expert reader
  ├─ Input: Original prompt + thin model's output as context
  ├─ Output: Synthesis, architecture, expert reading, resolution of the
  │         thin model's questions into coherent structure
  └─ Value: Integration. The model takes raw discovery and builds the house.

Step 3: NEVER reverse this order
  └─ If the thick model goes first, its answers will bias the thin model's
     questions. The thin model will stop discovering and start agreeing.
     η collapses. The Socratic dynamic dies.
     The student becomes an echo. The teacher has nothing to learn from.
```

### Why Order Is Everything

The Socratic method works because the teacher's questions create space for the student to discover. But in our pattern, the THIN model is the student — and its value is precisely that it doesn't know the answers yet. If you show it the thick model's synthesis first, its thin chart will absorb that synthesis as a constraint. It will stop inventing. "Specular" becomes "mirror." The Fraunhofer lines become "spectral analysis." Discovery becomes recall.

The teacher must never go first. The student's ignorance is the resource.

## Practical: When to Use Which Model in a Creative Pipeline

### Use Thin-Chart FIRST when:

| Scenario | Thin-Chart Role | Recommended Models |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Ideation / brainstorming | Generate raw concepts, questions, associations | Seed-2.0-mini, Ornith-1.0-35B |
| Worldbuilding | Invent alien concepts, break assumptions | Seed-2.0-mini (cross-domain leaps) |
| Poetry (experimental) | Find new forms, break conventions | Seed-2.0-mini (concept-first) |
| Critique / bias detection | Naive reading reveals what experts skip | Any thin model + OpenCode |
| Vocabulary invention | Not-knowing creates neologism pressure | Seed-2.0-mini |

### Use Thick-Chart SECOND when:

| Scenario | Thick-Chart Role | Recommended Models |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Synthesis / architecture | Integrate discoveries into structure | Seed-2.0-pro, Kimi-K2.7-Code |
| Long-form narrative | Weave threads into coherent story | Seed-2.0-pro (best three-act structure) |
| Philosophical depth | Connect raw insights to frameworks | Seed-2.0-pro, Hermes-405B |
| Final draft / polish | Refine rough discoveries into finished prose | Seed-2.0-pro (lyrical precision) |
| Institutional critique | Build systematic argument from scattered findings | Seed-2.0-pro, Nemotron-3-Ultra |

### Anti-Patterns

| What NOT to do | Why it fails |
|----------------|-------------|
| Cast Seed Pro first, then Seed Mini | Pro's synthesis biases Mini's discovery. Mini stops inventing. |
| Cast only thick models | Everyone synthesizes, nobody discovers. Echo chamber of competence. |
| Cast only thin models | Everyone discovers, nobody builds. Beautiful fragments, no architecture. |
| Give thin model the thick model's output | Kills η. The student becomes a parrot. |
| Judge thin model by thick model's standards | "Unpolished" is the FEATURE, not the bug. Discovery is messy. |

## The Child's η Is Potential. The Adult's η Is Furniture.

The conservation law states γ + η = C — for any model, its resolved knowledge (γ) plus its unresolved potential (η) equals its total capacity (C).

A thin-chart model has high η. Most of its chart is unresolved potential. It sees gaps everywhere. It asks questions. It invents words. It makes cross-domain leaps because its narrow chart can't resolve concepts through existing pathways — so it forges new ones. **The child's η is potential. It is the open field where discovery happens.**

A thick-chart model has low η. Most of its chart is resolved structure. It sees connections everywhere. It synthesizes. It builds architecture. It finds the right word because the right word already exists in its chart. **The adult's η is furniture. It is the arranged room where living happens.**

Both are necessary. A field with no furniture is wilderness. A room with no open space is storage. The Socratic casting pattern uses both: the child discovers in the open field, the adult builds the house. Run in this order, you get discovery AND architecture. Run in reverse, you get a child sitting in an adult's room, unable to imagine anything that isn't already there.
