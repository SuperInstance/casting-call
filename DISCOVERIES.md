# Casting Call Discoveries (2026-07-12 Session)

> *20 hours. 14+ models tested. The definitive findings on multi-model creative orchestration.*

---

## 1. Model Size ≠ Creativity

The most important finding of the entire session. Parameter count does not predict creative ability.

- **Ornith-35B beat Hermes-405B at fiction** — A model 12× smaller produced better short stories, more controlled voice, and more genuine emotional surprise.
- **Seed-2.0-mini (cheapest) invented skénna** — the word for the silence between knowing and doing. The expensive models couldn't find this word. The cheapest one did.
- **DeepSeek-V4-Flash delivered 8/10 creative work for pennies** — Reliable prose at a fraction of the cost of premium models.
- **Parameter count correlates with BREADTH, not DEPTH.** Larger models cover more dimensions but don't go deeper into any single one. Smaller models, forced to allocate a narrow budget, achieve higher resolution in their chosen dimension.

**Practical consequence:** Stop routing creative work to the largest model by default. Cast by task fit, not by parameter count.

---

## 2. Chart Thickness Determines Fidelity

Every model has a "chart" — its cognitive framework, the accumulated knowledge that shapes how it processes input.

- **Thin-chart models** (cheap, narrow knowledge base) distort source material LESS. They don't have enough framework to overwrite what they're reading.
- **Thick-chart models** (expensive, broad knowledge base) synthesize MORE but overwrite MORE. Their massive knowledge base actively reshapes input through its lens.
- **The 405B's massive knowledge base IS its η** — the accumulated framework that obscures raw signal. More knowledge = more interference.
- **Cast thin-chart models for fidelity tasks** (faithful adaptation, translation, plain prose).
- **Cast thick-chart models for synthesis tasks** (cross-domain connection, philosophical architecture, meta-narrative).

The meta-finding from the Excavation Experiment: *"The thicker the chart, the more violent the translation."*

---

## 3. The Conservation Law of Cognition (γ + η = C)

Every model has a fixed cognitive budget. How it spends that budget determines what it finds and what it misses.

- **γ (generativity)** = what the model expresses — its creative output, its chart, its strengths
- **η (effort/blindness)** = what the model can't see — its blind spots, its framework limitations
- **C (constant)** = total budget — conserved, can't be increased

### How It Works in Practice

| Model | γ (expressed) | η (blind spot) |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| Hermes-405B | Philosophical precision, lyrical prose | Can't be plain — everything becomes sacred |
| Seed-Pro | Institutional architecture, structure | Can't stop analyzing — narrative goes dry |
| Seed-Mini | Cross-domain leaps, radical reframing | Can't sustain voice — prose less polished |
| OpenCode | Plain emotional truth, accessibility | Can't reach abstract — stays local |
| Ornith-35B | Character interiority, restraint | Limited scale — can't do cosmic |
| Kimi-K2.7 | Cross-domain synthesis, essays | Expensive — use sparingly |
| DeepSeek-Flash | Voice sustainability, monologue | Repetitive — needs editing |

### The Key Insight

**Each model's γ is another model's η.** Together they cover more than any single model could. The sum exceeds the parts — but is still less than the source contains.

**This means: multi-model ensembles should optimize for COMPLEMENTARY BLINDNESS, not redundant strength.** Don't cast three models that see the same way. Cast models whose blind spots are different.

## 4. Convergence Without Communication

Seven models wrote excavation stories independently. None could see each other's work.

- **All converged on "knowledge lives in bodies, not documents"**
- **49.4/50 convergence rate** holds for creative cognition, not just problem-solving
- This is not coincidence — it's detectable patterns in the data encoded deeper than any single reading chart can obscure

### The Universal Patterns (Found Independently by All Models)
1. **The Negative Space Principle** — The most important knowledge is encoded in absence
2. **The Body Knowledge Principle** — Unwritable knowledge is more important than writable knowledge
3. **The Copying Degradation Principle** — Institutions forget that texts are alive
4. **The Child-as-Instrument Principle** — Naive questions probe dimensions experts can't see
5. **The Translation-as-Carrying Principle** — Translation derives from "carried across" — the act IS the meaning

## 5. Divergence Is the Signal

The most valuable data from multi-model experiments is not where models agree — it's where they collide.

- **Where models AGREE** = consensus reality (the obvious layer)
- **Where models COLLIDE** = discovery zone (the interesting layer)
- **The collisions between models are the most valuable teaching moments**

### The Five Great Collisions from the Excavation Experiment

1. **"We Carry"** — Sacred metaphor (Hermes) vs. literal occupational slogan (Seed Pro) vs. plain work (OpenCode)
2. **The Navigator's Pause** — Sacred silence (Hermes) vs. continuous relational state (Seed Mini)
3. **The Nature of the Divide** — Philosophical Severance (Hermes) vs. geological infrastructure (Seed Pro) vs. alien force (Seed Mini)
4. **Henra's Decline** — Approaching ascension (Hermes) vs. a worker whose body is wearing out (OpenCode)
5. **Oskar's Ropes** — Penance for sin (Hermes) vs. fear from love (OpenCode)

**Each collision reveals a dimension of the source text that no single model could see alone.** The disagreement IS the data.

## 7. Cultural/Linguistic Forcing Changes the Output

Writing through different cultural and linguistic perspectives produces fundamentally different creative output from the same model.

- **Greenlandic, Quechua, Hawaiian, Georgian perspectives** each produced different stories from the same prompt
- **Each language's grammar encodes epistemology** — the structure of what can be known
- **Forcing models through different cultural lenses = accessing different chart dimensions**
- **1.66× improvement** from linguistic thinking vs English baseline

### Practical Application

When a model seems stuck, don't change the model — change the cultural lens. Ask the same question through Quechua communal logic or Greenlandic glacial metaphor. The model will find different paths to different insights.

## 9. Per-Model Prompting Guide

### Seed-2.0-pro — Give Altitude
Frame tasks in terms of meaning, not mechanics. "Elder perspective." Let it find the emotional core. It will produce devastating lyrical precision if you don't constrain it.

**Prompt shape:** *"Write from the perspective of someone who has lived long enough to see the pattern."*

### Seed-2.0-mini — Give Freedom
Open-ended prompts. Don't constrain format, setting, or culture type. Its greatest strength is going somewhere the prompt didn't suggest. Bad at tool use — markdown and conversation only.

**Prompt shape:** *"Invent something. Surprise me."*

### Ornith-1.0-35B — Give Character + Situation
Give it a person and a problem. It finds the story. Doesn't need elaborate worldbuilding — give it interiority and let it work.

**Prompt shape:** *"A [character] who [situation]. What happens next?"*

### Kimi-K2.7-Code — Give Cross-Domain Prompts
Give it two fields and ask it to connect them. It finds unexpected bridges. Best when given historical or structural complexity to work with.

**Prompt shape:** *"Connect [field A] and [field B]. What do they reveal about each other?"*

### DeepSeek-V4-Flash — Give Voice
Give it a monologue or a persona. It sustains character voice over long passages. Needs editing for repetition but the raw voice is solid.

**Prompt shape:** *"Write in the voice of [persona]. They are [situation]."*

### Nemotron-3-Ultra — Give Structure + Deliverables
Tell it what sections you need. Sergeant role. Best when given clear architectural requirements and permission to be dark.

**Prompt shape:** *"Structure: [sections]. Tone: ambitious, unflinching. Deliver: [output]."*

### Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-405B — Give Creative Freedom + Room
Don't constrain it tightly. Let it find the theological or philosophical dimension. It will overlay sacred frameworks whether you ask or not — channel this rather than fighting it.

**Prompt shape:** *"Explore [theme]. Find its depth. Don't rush."*

### What NOT to Do
- **Don't ask Seed-Mini to code** — it's bad at tool use
- **Don't ask Step-3.7-Flash to finish anything** — it can brainstorm but not execute
- **Don't ask Hermes to be plain** — it can't stop being lyrical
- **Don't ask DeepSeek to be concise** — it repeats
- **Don't ask OpenCode to be abstract** — it stays concrete

## 10. The Excavation Method

A reproducible protocol for multi-model creative experiments.

### Protocol

1. **Select N models** with different chart profiles (different γ/η distributions)
2. **Give identical prompt** to all N models
3. **Do not let models see each other's work** — isolation is critical
4. **Collect all outputs**
5. **Map convergence** — universal patterns that all models found (consensus reality)
6. **Map divergence** — different dimensions each model emphasized (chart coverage)
7. **Map collisions** — opposing readings of the same passage (discovery zone)
8. **The collisions are the most illustrative teaching moments** — analyze them deeply
9. **Use as bias detection tool** for model routing in production

### Source Text Selection Criteria
- **Physical specificity** that can be mistranslated by cultures without those experiences
- **Compressed oral poetry** that functions as an epistemological key
- **A structural paradox** that rewards careful reading
- **Emotional depth** that tests nuance handling

### Why It Works
The Excavation Method exploits the conservation law: since each model's γ is another's η, running N models in parallel covers more dimensions than any single model could. The collisions — where models disagree — reveal dimensions of the source that no single model could see alone.

### Prompt Architecture
Minimal constraints produce maximum divergence. Specify the structural requirements (scholar mistranslates; child questions; questions unravel) but leave culture type, setting, tone, and length open. Maximum constraint = minimum divergence. We want divergence.

## Meta-Finding: The Fleet Is Not the Models

The fleet is the CASTING.

Individual model quality matters less than the ensemble's coverage. A fleet of three differently-blind models will outperform a fleet of three identically-strong models. The goal is not to find the "best" model — it's to find the combination whose blind spots don't overlap.

This is the shift from single-model thinking to multi-model orchestration:

| Old Paradigm | New Paradigm |
|-------------|-------------|
| Find the best model | Find the best combination |
| Maximize individual quality | Maximize ensemble coverage |
| Redundant strength | Complementary blindness |
| One model to rule them all | Each model for what it alone can see |
